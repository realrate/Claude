## Load
- `context/brand-core.md`
- `context/brand-voice.md`

---

## Steps
1. Verify all ECR data at realrate-archive.com
2. Build 8–10 slide PDF following structure below
3. Apply design rules
4. Write caption
5. Upload to LinkedIn as a Document post

---

## Slide Structure

| Slide | Content |
|---|---|
| **Cover** | "[Industry] Financial Health Report [Year] — What the Data Reveals" |
| **2** | Industry snapshot: total companies ranked, industry average ECR, number Top-Rated |
| **3** | The top tier — top 5 companies with ECR numbers and shared dominant driver |
| **4** | The biggest mover — rank change, ECR before/after, driver that caused it |
| **5** | The surprise — a well-known company whose ECR contradicts assumptions |
| **6** | The warning signal — bottom tier fragility pattern with named companies |
| **7** | What separates Top-Rated from the rest — the structural insight |
| **8** | What this means for [target reader] — CFO, risk officer, or investor |
| **9** | Methodology note — how ECR works, what makes it explainable and independent |
| **10** | CTA — "View the full [year] [industry] ranking at realrate.ai" |

### Optional Extended Slides (add when data warrants)
- Cross-industry comparison (when contrast reveals something meaningful)
- Multi-year ECR trend (when there's a clear directional story)
- Top-Rated consistency across years (when same names appear repeatedly)
- Full driver breakdown (when multiple drivers are significant)
- Macro context (high-stakes industries: insurance, pharma, finance)

Standard: 8–10 slides. Extended: up to 13–14. Never pad.

---

## Design Rules
- Every slide: RealRate logo consistent corner, header bar with industry name + year
- Data slides: semantic colors — green for strength, red for risk
- Text slides: Manrope, strong contrast, no grey text on white
- Cover: dark blue background `#00679B`, white text
- CTA slide: light background, realrate.ai URL prominent, tagline included
- No emojis on any slide

---

## Caption
> *"RealRate ranked [X] [industry] companies using [year] financial data. Here's what the full picture looks like — who ranked well, who didn't, what drove the difference, and what it signals for the industry."*
> + `realrate.ai/rankings/[industry]/[year]`
