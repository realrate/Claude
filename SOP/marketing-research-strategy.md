# Marketing Research & Strategy SOP

---

## Purpose

To ensure all marketing decisions at RealRate are grounded in accurate, up-to-date market intelligence. This SOP governs how research is conducted, documented, and applied across content strategy, outreach, competitive positioning, and campaign planning.

RealRate is pre-revenue and actively testing product-market fit — market research is not a one-time exercise but an ongoing process that feeds directly into content, messaging, and sales strategy.

---

## 1. Market Landscape & Sizing

### Objective
Understand the size, structure, and dynamics of the financial intelligence and AI-driven ratings market — and where RealRate sits within it.

### Research Steps

1. **Define the market** — Identify the specific segments RealRate operates in:
   - Financial health ratings & intelligence
   - Explainable / causal AI for finance
   - Supply chain risk intelligence
   - Investor intelligence platforms

2. **Identify market size** — Research TAM, SAM, SOM for each segment:
   - Use reports from: IBISWorld, Statista, Grand View Research, CB Insights
   - Cross-reference with LinkedIn Sales Navigator industry data
   - Look for fintech / AI in finance market reports (annual)

3. **Map industry trends** — Track macro shifts relevant to RealRate's positioning:
   - Regulatory pressure on rating agencies (post-2008 reforms)
   - Rise of explainable AI in finance
   - ESG and financial transparency demand
   - Supply chain risk visibility post-COVID

4. **Identify adjacent markets** — Markets RealRate could expand into:
   - Credit risk platforms
   - ESG rating / reporting tools
   - Procurement intelligence

5. **Document findings** — Update this section quarterly or when major market shifts occur

### Output
- Market sizing summary (TAM / SAM / SOM per segment)
- Key trends summary (3–5 bullet points per trend)
- Adjacent market opportunities list
- Source log with dates

## 2. Competitor Analysis

### Objective
Maintain a clear, current understanding of what competitors offer, how they position themselves, where they are weak, and how RealRate should differentiate in messaging and content.

### Research Steps

1. **Identify competitors** — Categorize by type:
   - Direct: same product category, same buyer
   - Indirect: different approach, same problem
   - Methodological: same AI approach, different product

2. **Profile each competitor** — For each, document:
   - What they do and who they serve
   - Pricing model (issuer-paid, buyer-paid, subscription, etc.)
   - Key strengths and weaknesses
   - How they position themselves (tone, claims, messaging)
   - Social media presence and content strategy
   - Recent news, funding, or product updates

3. **Map the gaps** — Identify what no competitor offers that RealRate does

4. **Monitor ongoing** — Set up Google Alerts or LinkedIn follows for each competitor; review quarterly

5. **Apply to content** — Use competitor weaknesses as content angles (contrarian takes, transparency posts, methodology posts)

### Competitor Profiles

---

**RapidRatings** *(Primary Competitor)*
- **What they do:** Financial health rating platform focused on supply chain and credit risk management
- **Who they serve:** Procurement teams, investors, financial institutions
- **Pricing model:** Buyer-paid subscription — companies don't pay to be rated
- **Strengths:** Established brand, strong supply chain market presence, large data set
- **Weaknesses:** Correlation-based (not causal), no driver explainability, paywalled data, no certification product, no public archive
- **Positioning:** Risk management tool for procurement and finance teams
- **Content gap:** Does not educate on *why* companies fail — only flags risk scores
- **Website:** https://www.rapidratings.com

---

**Moody's / S&P** *(Traditional Incumbent)*
- **What they do:** Nationally Recognized Statistical Rating Organizations (NRSROs) — credit ratings for bonds, companies, and structured products
- **Who they serve:** Capital markets, institutional investors, large corporates
- **Pricing model:** Issuer-paid — companies pay Moody's/S&P to receive a rating (core conflict of interest)
- **Strengths:** Brand authority, regulatory recognition, global scale, deeply embedded in capital markets
- **Weaknesses:** Issuer-paid = conflict of interest, not explainable, slow and expensive, inaccessible to mid-size companies, failed to flag major collapses (2008)
- **Positioning:** Authoritative, institutional, regulatory-grade
- **Content gap:** Cannot honestly claim independence or transparency — RealRate owns this positioning
- **Note:** RealRate was founded specifically in response to this model — Holger witnessed a CEO pressure a rating agency for an AAA rating
