# Marketing Research & Strategy SOP

---

## Purpose

To ensure all marketing decisions at RealRate are grounded in accurate, up-to-date market intelligence. This SOP governs how research is conducted, documented, and applied across content strategy, outreach, competitive positioning, and campaign planning.

RealRate is pre-revenue and actively testing product-market fit — market research is not a one-time exercise but an ongoing process that feeds directly into content, messaging, and sales strategy.

---

## 1. Market Landscape & Sizing

### Objective
Understand the size, structure, and dynamics of the financial intelligence and AI-driven ratings market — and where RealRate sits within it.

### Research Steps

1. **Define the market** — Identify the specific segments RealRate operates in:
   - Financial health ratings & intelligence
   - Explainable / causal AI for finance
   - Supply chain risk intelligence
   - Investor intelligence platforms

2. **Identify market size** — Research TAM, SAM, SOM for each segment:
   - Use reports from: IBISWorld, Statista, Grand View Research, CB Insights
   - Cross-reference with LinkedIn Sales Navigator industry data
   - Look for fintech / AI in finance market reports (annual)

3. **Map industry trends** — Track macro shifts relevant to RealRate's positioning:
   - Regulatory pressure on rating agencies (post-2008 reforms)
   - Rise of explainable AI in finance
   - ESG and financial transparency demand
   - Supply chain risk visibility post-COVID

4. **Identify adjacent markets** — Markets RealRate could expand into:
   - Credit risk platforms
   - ESG rating / reporting tools
   - Procurement intelligence

5. **Document findings** — Update this section quarterly or when major market shifts occur

### Output
- Market sizing summary (TAM / SAM / SOM per segment)
- Key trends summary (3–5 bullet points per trend)
- Adjacent market opportunities list
- Source log with dates

## 2. Competitor Analysis

### Objective
Maintain a clear, current understanding of what competitors offer, how they position themselves, where they are weak, and how RealRate should differentiate in messaging and content.

### Research Steps

1. **Identify competitors** — Categorize by type:
   - Direct: same product category, same buyer
   - Indirect: different approach, same problem
   - Methodological: same AI approach, different product

2. **Profile each competitor** — For each, document:
   - What they do and who they serve
   - Pricing model (issuer-paid, buyer-paid, subscription, etc.)
   - Key strengths and weaknesses
   - How they position themselves (tone, claims, messaging)
   - Social media presence and content strategy
   - Recent news, funding, or product updates

3. **Map the gaps** — Identify what no competitor offers that RealRate does

4. **Monitor ongoing** — Set up Google Alerts or LinkedIn follows for each competitor; review quarterly

5. **Apply to content** — Use competitor weaknesses as content angles (contrarian takes, transparency posts, methodology posts)

### Competitor Profiles

---

**RapidRatings** *(Primary Competitor)*
- **What they do:** Financial health rating platform focused on supply chain and credit risk management
- **Who they serve:** Procurement teams, investors, financial institutions
- **Pricing model:** Buyer-paid subscription — companies don't pay to be rated
- **Strengths:** Established brand, strong supply chain market presence, large data set
- **Weaknesses:** Correlation-based (not causal), no driver explainability, paywalled data, no certification product, no public archive
- **Positioning:** Risk management tool for procurement and finance teams
- **Content gap:** Does not educate on *why* companies fail — only flags risk scores
- **Website:** https://www.rapidratings.com

---

**Moody's / S&P** *(Traditional Incumbent)*
- **What they do:** Nationally Recognized Statistical Rating Organizations (NRSROs) — credit ratings for bonds, companies, and structured products
- **Who they serve:** Capital markets, institutional investors, large corporates
- **Pricing model:** Issuer-paid — companies pay Moody's/S&P to receive a rating (core conflict of interest)
- **Strengths:** Brand authority, regulatory recognition, global scale, deeply embedded in capital markets
- **Weaknesses:** Issuer-paid = conflict of interest, not explainable, slow and expensive, inaccessible to mid-size companies, failed to flag major collapses (2008)
- **Positioning:** Authoritative, institutional, regulatory-grade
- **Content gap:** Cannot honestly claim independence or transparency — RealRate owns this positioning
- **Note:** RealRate was founded specifically in response to this model — Holger witnessed a CEO pressure a rating agency for an AAA rating

## 3. Brand Positioning Strategy

### Objective
Define and maintain RealRate's position in the market — what it stands for, who it's for, and how it is distinct from every alternative.

### Positioning Statement
> RealRate is the **transparency-first financial intelligence platform** — the independent standard for assessing, ranking, and certifying financial health and stability using explainable AI.

### Positioning Pillars

| Pillar | What It Means | How It Differentiates |
|---|---|---|
| **Transparency** | Every rating is explainable — drivers, data, and logic shown | vs. Moody's/S&P: black-box, analyst-driven |
| **Independence** | Not issuer-paid. No conflicts of interest. | vs. Moody's/S&P: issuer-paid model |
| **Intelligence** | Causal AI — identifies what *drives* financial health, not just what correlates | vs. RapidRatings: correlation-based scoring |

> Note: Pillars are under review and may be updated.

### Messaging Framework

| Audience | Core Message | Proof Point |
|---|---|---|
| CFOs (Top-Rated) | "Display your financial strength with independent, explainable certification" | Top-Rated Seal + Bayerische testimonial |
| CFOs (Non Top-Rated) | "Understand exactly what's holding your financial rating back — and fix it" | Financial Health Report + Consulting |
| Risk Officers | "Stop relying on lagging indicators — see financial fragility before it becomes a problem" | ECR as leading indicator, 12-month early warning |
| Investors | "Go beyond the numbers — understand what actually drives the companies you invest in" | Investor Intelligence database |
| Procurement | "Vet your suppliers' financial health before disruption hits your supply chain" | Supply chain product, audited data only |

### Positioning Best Practices
- Always anchor claims in real ECR data — never make positioning claims without evidence
- Never directly attack competitors by name in public content
- Use competitor weaknesses as implicit contrast — let the data speak
- Reinforce independence and explainability in every piece of content
- Position RealRate as the standard, not a service — the way "Best Place to Work" is the standard

## 4. ICP & Audience Research Strategy

### Objective
Continuously refine understanding of who RealRate's best customers are, what drives their decisions, and how to reach them — especially while PMF is still being tested.

### Research Steps

1. **Analyse inbound engagement** — Who comments, shares, and DMs on LinkedIn? What roles, industries, company sizes?
2. **Review outreach replies** — Which ICPs reply? What language do they use? What pain points do they surface?
3. **Map product fit by ICP** — Which product resonates most with which buyer role?
4. **Interview or survey prospects** — When a lead engages, gather qualitative insights on their pain points and decision process
5. **Review Bayerische use case** — What made them a customer? What language did they use? Replicate that signal.
6. **Update `audience.md`** — Reflect findings in the audience context file quarterly

### ICP Priority Order *(current hypothesis — subject to revision)*
1. CFOs at Top-Rated eligible companies (Seal)
2. CFOs at non-top-rated companies (Consulting + Report)
3. Institutional investors (Investor Intelligence)
4. Supply chain / procurement leaders (Vendor Vetting)
5. SME / Private Equity (Seal + Workshop)

### Best Practices
- Treat all ICP assumptions as hypotheses until validated by actual customer behaviour
- Use content engagement as a signal — which pillars attract which roles?
- Use outreach reply data as a signal — which messaging gets responses?
- Never over-invest in an ICP that hasn't shown traction

## 6. Campaign Strategy

### Objective
Plan and execute marketing campaigns that move prospects through the funnel — from awareness to consideration to conversion — in a structured, measurable way.

### Campaign Types

| Campaign Type | Goal | Channel | Funnel Stage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ranking Launch | Awareness + reach | LinkedIn organic | TOFU |
| Industry Insight Series | Credibility + education | LinkedIn organic | MOFU |
| Risk Signal Campaign | Urgency + relevance for risk buyers | LinkedIn organic + ads | MOFU |
| Seal Awareness | Conversion — CFOs at eligible companies | LinkedIn organic + outreach | BOFU |
| Testimonial / Social Proof | Trust building | LinkedIn organic + outreach | BOFU |
| ICP Cold Outreach | Direct prospecting | Email (Instantly) + LinkedIn DM | All stages |

### Campaign Planning Process
1. Define the objective (awareness, lead gen, or conversion)
2. Identify the target ICP for this campaign
3. Select the channel(s)
4. Map content to funnel stage (TOFU / MOFU / BOFU)
5. Write content and design visuals
6. Set success metrics before launching
7. Run for minimum 4 weeks before evaluating
8. Document results and apply learnings to next campaign

### Best Practices
- One clear objective per campaign — never mix awareness and conversion in the same campaign
- Align campaign timing with ranking publication cycles where possible
- Always have a BOFU asset ready (archive link, methodology, testimonial) to support conversion
- Track every campaign against defined metrics — no campaigns without measurement
