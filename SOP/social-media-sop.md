# Social Media SOP

---

## Purpose

To establish a consistent, repeatable process for all RealRate social media activity — from content creation through design, scheduling, paid amplification, and performance review. This SOP ensures every piece of content is on-brand, data-grounded, and targeted at the right audience.

---

## 1. Content Creation & Copywriting

### Objective
Produce content that is analytical, credible, and relevant to RealRate's target audience (CFOs, risk officers, institutional investors) — always anchored in verified data.

### Best Practices
- Always verify ECR data at https://www.realrate-archive.com before writing
- Apply the one-line content filter before finalising any post: *"Would a CFO, risk officer, or institutional investor find this useful, credible, and distinctly RealRate?"*
- Never include unverified statistics — if it can't be confirmed, remove it or flag for review
- Write to the pillar distribution: 30% P1 / 25% P2 / 20% P3 / 15% P4 / 10% P5
- Match the copywriting framework to the pillar and goal (see below)

### Caption Formula
**Context → Insight → Implication**
- Context: What's the situation or data point?
- Insight: What does RealRate's analysis reveal?
- Implication: Why does this matter to the reader's role?

### Copywriting Frameworks by Use Case

| Framework | Structure | Use For |
|---|---|---|
| PAS | Problem → Agitate → Solve | P1 data drop posts |
| AIDA | Attention → Interest → Desire → Action | P3 explainer posts |
| Storytelling | Setup → Conflict → Resolution | P2 rankings posts |
| BAB | Before → After → Bridge | P5 testimonial / use case posts |
| Contrarian Take | Conventional Wisdom → Data → Reframe | P1 when ECR surprises |
| What / So What / Now What | Explain → Relevance → Action | P3 methodology / AI posts |
| Origin Story | Inciting Moment → Conflict → Decision | P4 — max 1–2× per quarter |

### Format Rules
- Max 2 emojis in caption, none on images
- No hashtags in captions
- Never use "we" in copy — always "RealRate"
- Always end the caption with the ranking URL: realrate.ai/rankings/[industry]/[year] or realrate.ai/methodology
- Tagline `"Powered by RealRate: Using Explainable Financial AI"` on the image only — never in the caption
- Never link to sales pages, pricing, or the archive — always link to realrate.ai/rankings/[industry]/[year] or realrate.ai/methodology
- Tag companies in first pinned comment only — never in caption

### Accuracy Rules
- RealRate data → verify at realrate-archive.com
- External data → verify against SEC filings, trusted publications, or official reports
- Big company names → only use what the data confirms
- When in doubt → leave it out

### Approval Workflow
1. Amneh drafts copy
2. Verify all data points against archive or source
3. Apply content filter
4. For company page posts → publish directly
5. For Holger's personal page → send to Holger for approval before scheduling

## 2. Content Calendar Planning

### Objective
Plan content at least 1–2 weeks ahead to maintain consistent cadence, balanced pillar distribution, and timely industry relevance.

### Best Practices
- Plan monthly, review weekly
- Map posts to pillar targets (30/25/20/15/10%) at the start of each month
- Reserve Wednesday slots for major ranking announcements
- Build in flexibility for timely/reactive posts (industry news, macro events)
- Coordinate ranking publication protocol separately (see ranking-publication-protocol.md)

### Posting Cadence

| Day | Content Type | Pillar |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Data reveal / ranking highlight | P1 |
| Wednesday | Insight / analysis / driver education | P2 or P3 |
| Friday | Risk signal / methodology / trust | P4 or P5 |
| Optional 4th | Industry news reaction | Any |

### Monthly Planning Steps
1. Review previous month's performance (what worked, what didn't)
2. Check archive for new or updated ranking data to anchor content
3. Map posts to pillars — ensure distribution targets are met
4. Draft post ideas with assigned frameworks and data sources
5. Flag any ranking publications and apply the ranking protocol
6. Schedule in Metricool or preferred scheduling tool

## 3. Evergreen Content Series (Between Ranking Cycles)

### Objective
Maintain a consistent 3–4 posts per week between ranking publication cycles using repeatable content templates — all grounded in verified archive data, no new ranking required.

Each series has a fixed template and cadence. Rotate through them to hit pillar distribution targets without starting from scratch each week.

---

### Series 1 — Driver Spotlight (P3)
**Cadence:** 1× every 2–3 weeks
**Format:** Single image
**Pillar:** P3 — Drivers of Financial Stability

**Template:**
1. What the driver is — one clear definition
2. Why it matters to financial health — the causal mechanism
3. What it looks like in top-rated vs. non-top-rated companies — archive data with ECR contribution figures
4. Implication for the reader's role (CFO, risk officer, investor)

**Drivers to cover (in rotation):**
- Stockholders' Equity
- Total Liabilities Ratio
- Cash & Equivalents
- Revenue vs. Asset Growth
- Debt Structure
- Operating Cash Flow Stability

Once all drivers are covered, restart the rotation with updated data.

---

### Series 2 — The Historical Signal (P4)
**Cadence:** Reactive — publish when a relevant company makes news
**Format:** Single image
**Pillar:** P4 — Risk & Warning Signals

**Trigger:** A company in RealRate's archive makes a significant headline — bankruptcy, credit downgrade, acquisition collapse, distress event.

**Template:**
1. The headline — what happened
2. What ECR showed 12–24 months before the event — specific numbers
3. The driver that was already signalling weakness
4. What this means for how you should assess financial health

**Rules:**
- Only use companies in RealRate's archive with verified ECR data
- Never speculate — let the contrast between ECR history and the outcome do the work
- This is the highest-credibility content type in the system — prioritise it whenever a news hook exists

---

### Series 3 — Cross-Industry Benchmark (P1/P2)
**Cadence:** 1× per month
**Format:** Single image or short carousel (3–4 slides)
**Pillar:** P1 or P2

**Template:**
1. Pick 2–3 industries from the archive
2. Compare average ECR — which is stronger and by how much
3. Name the dominant driver difference that explains the gap
4. Implication — what this tells investors or buyers about where structural strength sits

**Example hook:** *"US Software average ECR: 352%. US Pharma average: [X]%. The gap comes down to one structural difference."*

---

### Series 4 — The CFO Lens (P3)
**Cadence:** 1× every 2–3 weeks
**Format:** Single image
**Pillar:** P3 — Drivers of Financial Stability

**Template:**
1. One balance sheet concept from the CFO's perspective
2. What top-rated companies do differently on this metric — archive data
3. ECR contribution figures that prove it
4. One structural implication (observation, not advice)

**Topics in rotation:**
- Equity strength vs. liability growth
- Cash buffer ratios in the top tier
- Revenue vs. asset efficiency
- Liability management patterns in top-rated companies

**Audience:** CFOs of non-top-rated companies — educational, not critical

---

### Series 5 — The Investor/Risk View (P4)
**Cadence:** 1× every 2–3 weeks
**Format:** Single image
**Pillar:** P4 — Risk & Warning Signals

**Template:**
1. One financial health signal investors or risk officers miss using conventional metrics
2. What ECR reveals that credit ratings or revenue figures don't show
3. Archive data showing the gap between conventional signal and ECR reality
4. What to look for — practical and data-grounded

**Angle:** Credibility through differentiation — what independent, explainable AI reveals that issuer-paid ratings miss

---

### Series 6 — Structural Signals (P2/P4)
**Cadence:** 1× per month
**Format:** Single image
**Pillar:** P2 or P4

**Template:**
1. A financial health pattern visible in the archive data
2. What historically happens to companies that match this pattern — based on existing data, not speculation
3. Which companies in a given industry currently match the pattern — aggregate, never a negative company-specific call
4. Structural explanation of why this pattern precedes distress

**Framing rule:** Always historical and conditional — *"Companies with ECR below X and declining [driver] contribution have historically shown elevated risk in the following 12–18 months."* Never forecast a specific company's future. Never issue a speculative claim.

---

### Series 7 — Methodology & Independence (P5)
**Cadence:** 1× per month maximum
**Format:** Single image or portrait (text-heavy)
**Pillar:** P5 — Methodology & Trust

**Template:**
1. One thing RealRate does differently
2. Why traditional ratings miss this
3. What ECR reveals as a result
4. One named example from the archive where this difference showed up in the data

**Topics in rotation:**
- Why issuer-paid ratings are structurally compromised
- What "explainable AI" means in practice — not a black box
- What ECR measures that revenue growth doesn't
- Why RealRate refuses to sell ratings
- What independence means for a CFO, investor, or risk officer

---

### Series 8 — Industry in Focus (P2)
**Cadence:** Between ranking cycles — use already-published data
**Format:** Short carousel (3–4 slides)
**Pillar:** P2 — Insight & Interpretation

**Template:**
1. Pick an industry with a published ranking (not the current cycle)
2. Pull an angle not covered in the original publication cycle
3. Apply a new framing to the same data — different insight, different reader

**Example:** US Software was published 3 months ago → *"The companies that improved their ECR the most in US Software in 2025 — and what changed on their balance sheet."*

**Use when:** There is no current ranking cycle running and the calendar needs filling. Never recycle the same angle already used in the original cycle.

---

### Series 9 — The Newcomer Watch (P1/P2)
**Cadence:** After each ranking publication — use unpublished newcomer data from prior cycles
**Format:** Single image
**Pillar:** P1 or P2

**Template:**
1. A company that entered the Top-Rated list for the first time in any of RealRate's covered industries
2. What they were before (rank, ECR) vs. what they are now
3. The driver that got them there — named, with pp contribution
4. What this company's rise signals structurally for others in the industry

**Use when:** Between cycles — surface newcomer stories from industries published in prior months that didn't get a dedicated newcomer post.

---

### Series 10 — Small vs. Big (P1/P2)
**Cadence:** 1× per month
**Format:** Single image
**Pillar:** P1 or P2

**Template:**
1. A lesser-known or smaller company that outranks a well-known industry giant on ECR
2. Named comparison — both companies, both ECR scores
3. The structural driver difference that explains the gap
4. Implication: what this tells investors or buyers about the limits of name-recognition as a proxy for financial health

**Rule:** Frame positively — the smaller company's strength story, not the giant's failure story. Never name the larger company negatively.

---

### Series 11 — The Time Machine (P2)
**Cadence:** 1× per month
**Format:** Short carousel (3–4 slides) or single image
**Pillar:** P2 — Insight & Interpretation

**Template:**
1. Pick a company in RealRate's archive with 3+ years of data
2. Show ECR trajectory over time — a clear directional story
3. Identify the structural change that drove the trajectory (driver that strengthened or weakened consistently)
4. Implication: what multi-year stability or decline looks like vs. a single-year snapshot

**Why this works:** Single-year rankings tell you where a company is. Multi-year data tells you whether the balance sheet is building or eroding. That's a different and more valuable signal.

---

### Series 12 — Macro Lens (P2/P4)
**Cadence:** Reactive — when a macro event is relevant to ECR-covered industries
**Format:** Single image
**Pillar:** P2 or P4

**Trigger:** A significant external event — interest rate change, geopolitical event, regulatory shift, sector-wide disruption (AI adoption, energy shock, pandemic effect) — that is plausibly connected to ECR movements in a covered industry.

**Template:**
1. The macro event — brief, factual
2. How it shows up in ECR data across the affected industry — industry average movement, driver shifts
3. Which companies in the archive were most exposed or most insulated — named, with ECR data
4. What this means for buyers, investors, or CFOs assessing companies in this environment

**Rule:** Never speculate beyond what the data shows. Frame as "the data shows X happened" not "X will cause Y."

---

### Series 13 — The Causal Chain (P2/P3)
**Cadence:** 1× per month
**Format:** Single image or short carousel
**Pillar:** P2 or P3

**Template:**
1. Pick one company and one ranking cycle
2. State the outcome (ECR score, rank)
3. Walk backward through the causal chain: which driver was the root cause → what structural condition created that driver → why that condition exists in this company specifically
4. Show how ECR makes this chain visible — what traditional ratings or revenue metrics miss

**This series answers "why" — not just "what."** The causal relationship between financial structure and ECR outcome is RealRate's core differentiation. This series makes that explicit.

**Example structure:**
> *"[Company] ranked #1 in [Industry] with an ECR of [X]%. That number is the outcome. Here's the cause: [Driver] contributed +[Y] points — because [structural reason]. That structural condition exists because [business decision or model]. Traditional credit ratings don't surface this. ECR does."*

---

### Series 14 — Action Steps & Decision Support (P3/P4)
**Cadence:** 1× per month
**Format:** Single image
**Pillar:** P3 or P4

**Template:**
Pick a reader role (CFO, investor, risk officer, procurement) and give them one concrete decision-support insight grounded in ECR data.

| Reader | Angle |
|---|---|
| **CFO** | "Here's what the ECR data shows the top-tier companies in your industry are doing differently — and what your balance sheet can learn from it" |
| **Investor** | "Here's what ECR reveals about [industry] companies before it shows up in credit ratings or earnings reports" |
| **Risk officer** | "Here's the pattern that appears in ECR data before a company shows signs of distress — what to look for" |
| **Procurement** | "Here's how to use ECR as a vendor financial health screen — what the data shows about [industry] suppliers" |

**Rule:** Ground every action step in real archive data. No generic advice. Every "what to do" must connect to what the data actually shows.

---

### Series 15 — Validation & Credibility (P5)
**Cadence:** 1× per month maximum
**Format:** Single image
**Pillar:** P5 — Methodology & Trust

**This is the closest RealRate gets to promotional content — handled carefully per brand voice rules.**

**Template:**
1. A specific insight that RealRate's analysis revealed — named company, real ECR data, real driver
2. What happened afterward (if verifiable — company in the news, distress event, market movement)
3. How ECR surfaced this signal before it was publicly visible
4. What this means for how CFOs, investors, or risk officers should use independent financial AI

**Rule:** Never make it about RealRate the product. Make it about the insight — what the data revealed, why it matters, what the reader can act on. The credibility comes from the data, not from claiming RealRate is great.

**Frame as:** "Here's what the data showed. Here's what happened. Here's what it means for your decisions." — not "Use RealRate to get these insights."

## 4. LinkedIn Company Page Management

### Objective
Grow RealRate's LinkedIn presence among precision targets — CFOs, risk officers, institutional investors, actuaries — through consistent, credible, data-driven content.

### Best Practices
- Company page is the primary focus — Holger's page is secondary
- Post 3–4× per week minimum
- Always engage with comments within 24 hours
- Pin ranking posts when published — unpin after the publication cycle ends
- Monitor follower growth and audience composition monthly
- Aim to shift audience from broad engagement → precision targeting

### Posting Workflow
1. Draft caption using framework + content filter
2. Design visual (if required) per design specs
3. Verify all data points
4. Write first pinned comment (company tags only — link is already in the caption)
5. Schedule or post
6. Monitor engagement for 24–48 hours
7. Respond to all comments

### Engagement Rules
- Respond to all comments — even short acknowledgements
- Never pitch in comments — keep responses analytical and value-adding
- For inbound interest → move to DM (see outreach-sop.md)
- Tag companies in first pinned comment only (max 3–5)

## 5. Social Creative Design

### Objective
Produce on-brand, visually consistent LinkedIn graphics that reinforce RealRate's financial intelligence positioning.

### Best Practices
- Always use Manrope font — never substitute without approval
- Maintain strong contrast between background, text, and logo at all times
- Less is more — flat, minimal, data-focused aesthetic
- Check every design against the brand compliance checklist before publishing

### Design Workflow
1. Claude creates the design directly (HTML/CSS or Python image libraries)
2. Choose background — mix primary (`#00679B`) and secondary (`#f5f5f5`, `#e8e8e8`) based on what looks best for the content. Alternate across posts in a cycle for visual variety.
3. Set all text in Manrope
4. Place logo in whichever corner has the most available space and least visual conflict with content — no fixed corner
5. Add tagline on analytical post images: `"Powered by RealRate: Using Explainable Financial AI"` — image only, never in the caption
6. Review against compliance checklist
7. Export as PNG/PDF and attach to scheduled post

### Design Specs

| Format | Dimensions | Use For |
|---|---|---|
| Square | 1080 × 1080px | Feed posts, carousels (primary) |
| Portrait | 1080 × 1350px | Document-style, text-heavy posts |

### Typography Scale (LinkedIn)
| Element | Size |
|---|---|
| Main heading | 60–75px max |
| Subheadings | 28–45px |
| Body text (light slide) | 35px |
| Body text (dark slide) | 42px |
| Footer | 20–25px |

### Approved Backgrounds
- Primary: `#00679B` (dark blue)
- Secondary: `#f5f5f5` (light grey), `#e8e8e8` (medium grey)
- Mix primary and secondary across posts — choose whichever looks best for the content and creates visual variety within the cycle
- Minimal gradients and subtle textures permitted
- Must maintain clear contrast between background, text, and logo

### Brand Compliance Checklist
- [ ] Manrope font used throughout
- [ ] Logo placed in the corner with most available space — no fixed position
- [ ] No emojis on image
- [ ] Strong contrast between all elements
- [ ] Tagline on image (analytical posts) — not in caption
- [ ] No grey text on white or light backgrounds
- [ ] Colors from approved palette only
- [ ] Nothing overlapping or cut off

## 6. LinkedIn Ads Strategy

### Objective
Amplify RealRate's best-performing organic content and drive precision-targeted awareness among CFOs, risk officers, and institutional investors.

### Best Practices
- Never boost content that hasn't performed organically first
- Target by job title, seniority, and industry — not broad interest categories
- Use Document Ads for thought leadership content (high engagement for B2B)
- Start small — test with low budgets before scaling
- Define a clear objective per campaign (awareness, engagement, or lead gen) — never mix

### Current Status
LinkedIn Ads are active but lack a defined strategy. Priority is to establish structure before scaling spend.

### Campaign Structure

| Campaign Type | Objective | Format | Audience |
|---|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Brand visibility | Single image / video | CFOs, risk officers, investors by industry |
| Engagement | Content amplification | Document Ad / carousel | Same as above — retarget page visitors |
| Lead Gen | Demo / consultation requests | Lead Gen Form | Warm audience — retargeted from engagement |

### Targeting Approach
- **Job titles:** CFO, Head of Risk, Head of Treasury, VP Finance, Procurement Director, Chief Investment Officer
- **Seniority:** Director, VP, C-Suite
- **Industries:** Financial Services, Insurance, Software, Construction, Consulting
- **Location:** US primary, Germany secondary
- **Audience size:** Aim for 50k–200k per campaign — not too narrow, not too broad

### Creative Guidelines
- Use best-performing organic posts as ad creative
- Keep copy concise — hook in first line
- No direct sales language — lead with data and insight
- Always A/B test at least 2 creative variants per campaign

### Optimization Cadence
- Review performance weekly for first month
- Pause underperforming ads at 2× average CPC
- Scale budget on ads with CTR above benchmark
- Refresh creative every 4–6 weeks to avoid fatigue
