# Ranking Publication Protocol SOP

---

## Purpose

To ensure every new industry ranking published by RealRate is launched in a structured, high-impact way — maximising reach on publication day and sustaining engagement through insight and analysis in the days that follow.

---

## Content Approach — Storytelling With Data

Every post in this protocol follows the same principle: **the story opens, the data proves, the visual shows.**

- **Story** — the hook. Opens with a narrative tension: a company that rose or fell, a pattern that contradicts assumptions, a signal hiding in the numbers. Makes the reader want to know what happened and why.
- **Data** — the proof. ECR numbers, driver contributions, rank movements, industry averages. Never dropped — always present. This is what makes RealRate credible and distinct from opinion-based content.
- **Visual** — the evidence. The carousel or graphic carries the data in a scannable, shareable format. The caption tells the story; the visual shows the numbers.

**The formula for every post:**
> *Story hook → data that proves it → structural explanation → implication for the reader → tagline*

---

## Publication Cycle Overview

| Day | Post | Format | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 0 | Ranking carousel | Image carousel | Maximum reach — full top 10, all top-rated |
| Day +1 | Seal post | Single image | Organic amplification — company recognition |
| Day +2 | Insight 1 — Company Editorial | Single image | Most compelling company story from the ranking |
| Day +4 | Insight 2 — Industry YoY Shift | Carousel (up to 3 slides) | How the industry ECR moved year on year |
| Day +6 | Insight 3 — Rotating Angle | Single image | Broader industry analysis, data-driven angle |
| Day +8 | Insight 4 — NTR Rotating | Single image | Non-top-rated education and risk intelligence |
| Day +10 | Deep Dive | LinkedIn document (PDF, 8–10 slides) | Full ranking story in one piece |

> **Spacing rule:** Never post two ranking posts on consecutive days. Each post needs 48 hours minimum to gather engagement before the next goes out.

## Day 0 — Ranking Carousel

**Goal:** Maximum reach — the highest-priority post in the cycle
**Format:** Image carousel

### Rules
- Show full top 10 with ECR numbers
- Include industry average ECR for context
- Note biggest movers (up and down)
- Highlight all top-rated companies
- Pin this post immediately after publishing
- Always end the caption with the ranking URL: realrate.ai/rankings/[industry]/[year]

### Carousel Structure
| Slide | Content |
|---|---|
| Cover | "202X [Industry] Financial Health Ranking" — industry name and year prominent |
| Slides 2–4 | Top 10 companies with ECR numbers and rank vs. prior year |
| Slide 5 | Industry average ECR + number of top-rated companies |
| Final slide | Tagline + RealRate logo |

### Caption Structure
| Element | What it does | How to write it |
|---|---|---|
| **Story hook** | Opens with a fact that creates immediate curiosity | Name a company or a number that surprises — make the reader ask "why?" |
| **Data** | Top 10 with ECR numbers + industry average | Clean list — let the numbers speak |
| **One insight** | The most notable finding | One sentence — name the company or pattern, state the implication |
| **Ranking URL** | End of caption | `realrate.ai/rankings/[industry]/[year]` |

## Day +1 — Seal Post

**Goal:** High-engagement recognition post — the most shareable asset in the cycle
**Format:** Single image

### Design
- White or light grey background (#f5f5f5)
- RealRate Top-Rated seal: large, centred or prominently anchored
- All top-rated company logos arranged around the seal in a balanced grid or arc
- If too many logos to fit cleanly: feature top 12–15, caption references the full list
- No ECR numbers on this image — this is recognition, not analysis
- RealRate logo in the corner with most available space
- No tagline — this post is not analytical

### Caption
> *"Congratulations to the [year] Top-Rated [Industry] companies — ranked by independent, explainable financial AI."*

Short. Celebratory. No analytical copy — let the visual do the work.

### First Pinned Comment
> [tag every top-rated company — multiple comments if needed]

### Amplification Tasks — Day +1
- [ ] Tag all top-rated companies in pinned comments (multiple comments if needed — max 5 tags per comment)
- [ ] Comment tagging employees of top-rated companies to encourage sharing

- Companies see their logo and share it with their own audience — organic amplification at zero cost
- Tagging in comments creates notification chains across multiple companies
- Feels like an award, not a product post — companies engage, not scroll past

## Day +2 — Insight 1: Company Editorial

**Goal:** The most compelling individual company story from the ranking — makes a reader stop and want to understand why
**Format:** Single image

### Angle Selection

Look at the data and pick the angle most clearly supported. Decide before drafting.

| Angle | When the data shows this |
|---|---|
| **Reputable, Not Top-Rated** | A well-known company failed to reach Top-Rated — their ECR contradicts their reputation |
| **Unknown, Top-Rated** | A lesser-known company made Top-Rated ahead of recognized names in the same industry |
| **The Surprise** | Any non-obvious finding — a company's score, position, driver, or trajectory that defies expectation |

### Title Rule
Free-form. Written for this specific company and data. Professional, direct, one hook-line. Not the angle name.

### Visual
| Element | What to show |
|---|---|
| Company name | Prominent |
| Rank and ECR | Key data point — score or position |
| Key driver | Name + point contribution |
| Industry average | For context |
| RealRate logo | Any corner, 50px margin |
| Tagline | Bottom |

### Caption Structure
| Element | How to write it |
|---|---|
| **Story hook** | Lead with the company and the contrast — create the "why?" in the reader's mind |
| **Data** | Company name, rank, ECR score, driver contribution in points, industry average |
| **Interpretation** | What is driving the ECR result and why it matters |
| **Clarifying line** | *"ECR measures balance sheet strength, not revenue performance or market reputation."* — mandatory for Reputable Not Top-Rated |
| **Implication** | What this means for CFOs, investors, or risk officers reading it |
| **Ranking URL** | Always last — realrate.ai/rankings/[industry]/[year] |

### Rules
- One company, one driver, one implication — never dilute
- Always quantify the driver contribution in points
- For Reputable Not Top-Rated: never frame as criticism — it's a data distinction
- Two-company comparison layout approved for Unknown Top-Rated
- End caption with ranking URL

## Day +4 — Insight 2: Industry YoY Shift

**Goal:** Show how the industry ECR moved vs the prior 1–2 years — industry-wide or anchored on a well-known company
**Format:** Carousel — up to 3 slides

### Option Selection

| Option | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Industry-Wide** | The sector shift is the headline — average ECR moved, companies shifted, something structural changed |
| **Company-Level** | A well-known company's YoY shift is the story, supported by broader industry context |

### Slide Structure

**Slide 1 — The Shift**
Lead with the headline number. Industry ECR avg then vs now — or company ECR then vs now. The change in points is the visual hero.

**Slide 2 — What Drove It**
The key structural reason behind the shift. One finding, clearly stated.

**Slide 3 — The Context** *(only if it adds meaningful information)*
Where this industry sits vs others, or what the shift signals for the next cycle.

### Caption Structure
| Element | How to write it |
|---|---|
| **Story hook** | Lead with the number that changed — the shift in points or % |
| **Data** | ECR avg before and after, point change, key driver, # companies (if changed) |
| **Interpretation** | Why the shift happened — structural cause, not just the number |
| **Implication** | What it signals for the industry going forward |
| **Ranking URL** | Always last — realrate.ai/rankings/[industry]/[year] |

### Rules
- Max 3 slides — keep it tight
- Each slide carries one message — no overloading
- Slide numbering visible on every slide (1/3, 2/3, 3/3)
- Consistent background and typography across slides
- End caption with ranking URL

## Day +6 — Insight 3: Rotating Angle

**Goal:** Broader industry analysis — zoom out from a single company to a data-driven pattern, trend, or structural observation
**Format:** Single image

### Angle Selection

Rotate through the bank — pick the angle most supported by the current data and not used in the prior 2 ranking cycles.

| Angle | When to use |
|---|---|
| **The Industry Blind Spot** | One weakness driver appearing across most companies in the industry — a structural drag most haven't addressed |
| **The Market vs The Balance Sheet** | A company the market rates highly has a low ECR, or vice versa — perception vs structural health |
| **The Industry Trend** | Industry ECR has shifted over 2–3 years — getting stronger or weaker, and what's behind it |
| **The Methodology Moment** | What ECR measures that other ratings don't — one clear explanation that builds credibility |
| **The Investor Signal** | Is this industry financially healthy right now? Safe, risky, stable, fragile — the ECR answers it for investors and business partners |
| **The Macro Lens** | An industry under pressure from a continuous trend — war, sanctions, tariffs, rate cycles — what the balance sheets show that headlines don't |

### Caption Structure
| Element | How to write it |
|---|---|
| **Story hook** | Lead with the pattern or signal — specific to this data |
| **Data** | The numbers that prove the angle — ECR figures, driver data, industry averages |
| **Interpretation** | What the pattern means structurally |
| **Implication** | What it means for investors, CFOs, or business partners reading it |
| **Ranking URL** | Always last — realrate.ai/rankings/[industry]/[year] |

### Rules
- External claims (market data, macro events) must be sourced from trusted public sources — flag if unverifiable
- For Investor Signal and Macro Lens: always anchor on ECR data first, external context second
- End caption with ranking URL

## Day +8 — Insight 4: Non-Top-Rated Rotating

**Goal:** Education and risk intelligence — speaks to companies that didn't earn Top-Rated status, and to investors and partners evaluating them
**Format:** Single image

### Angle Selection

Rotate through the bank — pick the angle most clearly supported by the data.

| Angle | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Almost There** | A company sitting just below the Top-Rated threshold. One driver is the specific difference |
| **The One Thing** | A single weakness driver is pulling ECR below threshold — remove it and they'd qualify |
| **The Fallen** | A company that held Top-Rated in a prior year and has since dropped — what changed |
| **The Sector Drag** | Multiple companies in the same sub-sector all failing to reach Top-Rated — company problem or structural industry issue |
| **Close But Not Rated** | Performs well by traditional metrics — revenue, market cap, brand — but ECR threshold not met |
| **The Due Diligence Gap** | Why NTR status matters for investors, counterparties, and partners — anchored on a real company |

### Caption Structure
| Element | How to write it |
|---|---|
| **Story hook** | State the specific situation — company name or pattern — without framing it as criticism |
| **Data** | ECR score, rank, gap to threshold, key weakness driver + contribution, industry average |
| **Interpretation** | What the balance sheet shows and why it matters |
| **Implication** | Speak to the CFO (what to address) or the investor/partner (what to watch) |
| **Clarifying line** | *"ECR measures balance sheet strength, not revenue performance or market reputation."* — mandatory on all NTR posts |
| **Ranking URL** | Always last — realrate.ai/rankings/[industry]/[year] |

### Rules
- Always quantify — give the ECR score, the gap, the driver contribution in points
- For Almost There and The One Thing: name the company. The data is specific and constructive
- For The Sector Drag: industry-aggregate — no individual company names
- Caption clarifying line is non-negotiable on every NTR post
- End caption with ranking URL

## Day +9 or +10 — Deep Dive

**Goal:** Pull the full ranking story together in one comprehensive piece
**Format:** LinkedIn Document Carousel (PDF — 8 to 10 slides)

Document posts receive higher organic reach than image carousels on LinkedIn, allow deeper educational content, and are saved and reshared more. This is the most educational asset in the cycle — it serves the audience that wants the full picture.

### Slide Structure
| Slide | Content |
|---|---|
| **Cover** | "[Industry] Financial Health Report 2025 — What the Data Reveals" |
| **Slide 2** | Industry snapshot: total companies ranked, industry average ECR, number of top-rated |
| **Slide 3** | The top tier — top 5 companies with ECR numbers and shared dominant driver |
| **Slide 4** | The biggest mover — rank change, ECR before/after, driver that caused it |
| **Slide 5** | The surprise — a well-known company whose ECR contradicts assumptions |
| **Slide 6** | The warning signal — bottom tier fragility pattern with named companies |
| **Slide 7** | What separates top-rated from the rest — the structural insight |
| **Slide 8** | What this means for [target reader] — CFO, risk officer, investor |
| **Slide 9** | Methodology note — how ECR works, what makes it explainable and independent |
| **Slide 10** | CTA — "View the full [year] [industry] ranking at realrate.ai" |

### Optional Extended Slides (add when data is rich or industry is high-stakes)

| Slide | Content | When to add |
|---|---|---|
| **Cross-Industry Comparison** | How this industry's average ECR compares to 2–3 other RealRate-covered industries | When the comparison reveals something meaningful — unusually strong or fragile relative to peers |
| **Multi-Year Trend** | Industry average ECR over 3+ years — directional chart | When there's a clear upward or downward trend worth highlighting |
| **Top-Rated Consistency** | Companies that have held Top-Rated status across multiple years | When the same names appear consistently — shows structural reliability, not cyclical performance |
| **Full Driver Breakdown** | All major ECR drivers across the top tier — average contribution per driver | When multiple drivers are significant, not just one dominant one |
| **Macro Context** | How external conditions (interest rates, sector growth, regulation) connect to ECR movements | High-stakes industries (insurance, pharma, finance) where macro factors visibly moved the data |

> Standard Deep Dive: 8–10 slides (core structure). Extended: up to 13–14 slides (add relevant optional slides only — don't pad).

### Design Rules
- Every slide: RealRate logo in a consistent corner (whichever avoids content collision), consistent header bar with industry name and year
- Data slides: semantic colors — green for strength, red/warning for risk
- Text slides: Manrope, strong contrast, no grey text on white
- Cover: dark blue background (#00679B), white text
- CTA slide: light background, realrate.ai URL prominent, tagline included
- No emojis on any slide

### Caption
> *"RealRate ranked [X] [industry] companies using 2025 financial data. Here's what the full picture looks like — who ranked well, who didn't, what drove the difference, and what it signals for the industry."*

## Insight & Angle Selection Guide

Run this at the start of every ranking cycle before drafting any copy.

**Step 1 — Pull from the archive:**
- All top-rated companies with ECR numbers
- Full top 10 with rank vs. prior year
- Industry average ECR — current year and prior 1–2 years
- Greatest strength driver + contribution (pp) for each top company
- Greatest weakness driver for each company in the bottom third
- Top-Rated threshold ECR

**Step 2 — Select Insight 1 angle (Day +2 — Company Editorial):**

Check all three options and pick the strongest.

| If the data shows... | Use this angle |
|---|---|
| A well-known company failed to reach Top-Rated and their ECR clearly contradicts their market reputation | Reputable, Not Top-Rated |
| A lesser-known company made Top-Rated ahead of recognized industry names by a striking margin | Unknown, Top-Rated |
| Any other non-obvious finding — a rank, score, driver, or trajectory that defies expectation | The Surprise |

**Step 3 — Select Insight 2 option (Day +4 — YoY Shift):**

| If the data shows... | Use this option |
|---|---|
| Significant ECR average shift at the industry level | Industry-Wide |
| A well-known company's year-over-year ECR shift is the more compelling story | Company-Level |

**Step 4 — Select Insight 3 angle (Day +6 — Rotating):**

Pick from the bank. Must not have been used in the prior 2 ranking cycles.

| If the data shows... | Use this angle |
|---|---|
| One weakness driver appearing across most companies | The Industry Blind Spot |
| A company the market rates highly has a low ECR, or vice versa | The Market vs The Balance Sheet |
| Industry ECR has shifted meaningfully over 2–3 years | The Industry Trend |
| No single data angle — need to build credibility around the methodology | The Methodology Moment |
| Industry health is a question for investors or business partners | The Investor Signal |
| Industry affected by a macro trend — geopolitical, economic, regulatory | The Macro Lens |

**Step 5 — Select Insight 4 angle (Day +8 — NTR Rotating):**

| If the data shows... | Use this angle |
|---|---|
| A company just below the Top-Rated threshold with one specific driver gap | Almost There |
| One weakness driver pulling a company below threshold | The One Thing |
| A company that was Top-Rated in a prior year and has dropped | The Fallen |
| Multiple companies in the same sub-sector all failing to reach Top-Rated | The Sector Drag |
| A company that looks strong by traditional metrics but fails the ECR threshold | Close But Not Rated |
| An opportunity to explain why NTR status matters to investors and partners | The Due Diligence Gap |

## PR & Articles

**Default: 1 article per ranking cycle.**
Publish a second only if the industry is high-stakes (insurance, pharma, finance) or the data reveals something exceptional.

Articles are published on the RealRate website or news.realrate.ai. Not reports — articles are shorter, more timely, and easier to produce consistently across multiple industries.

### Rotating Article Angles

Pick 1 (or 2 for high-stakes industries) per cycle based on what the data supports most strongly.

**Article 1 — Default (1 per cycle):**

| Angle | When to use | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| **Ranking Interpretation** | Every industry — good default | Overview of the full ranking, most surprising finding, what the data reveals |
| **Performance Drivers** | When the dominant driver is clear and compelling | Why the top companies ranked well — causal analysis of ECR drivers |
| **Year-over-Year Analysis** | When industry average ECR shifted significantly | What changed from the prior ranking and the structural reason behind it |
| **Industry Financial Health Snapshot** | First time publishing for an industry, or after a major market event | Big-picture health of the industry — how it compares to others RealRate covers |
| **The Outlier Story** | One company's ECR dramatically defies expectations | Named company, what drove it, what others in the industry can learn from the structural difference |
| **The Threshold Analysis** | Several companies sit close to the top-rated cutoff | How close non-top-rated companies are to the threshold and what one structural change would cross the line |
| **Sector Comparison** | RealRate covers multiple industries with published data | How this industry's average ECR compares to other covered industries — what the cross-sector pattern reveals |

**Article 2 — High-Stakes Industries Only (insurance, pharma, finance):**

| Angle | When to use | Reader |
|---|---|---|
| **Risk & Warning Signals** | Bottom-tier fragility pattern is striking | Investors, risk officers, procurement |
| **The Independence Angle** | Industry dominated by issuer-paid ratings | CFOs, institutional buyers |
| **The Buyer's Shortlist** | Data clearly separates companies by financial stability | Procurement and vendor vetting teams — which companies passed financial due diligence and why |
| **The Regulatory Lens** | Regulated industry where financial health maps to compliance exposure | Risk officers, compliance teams — how ECR aligns with regulatory vulnerability |
| **The Concentration Risk** | Top-rated tier is thin relative to total companies ranked | Investors with broad industry exposure — what it means when most of the sector sits in non-top-rated territory |

### Selection Logic

| Industry type | Articles per cycle | Article 1 angle | Article 2 angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| High-stakes (insurance, pharma, finance) | 2 | Ranking Interpretation (default) | Risk & Warning Signals or The Independence Angle |
| Standard (software, consulting, advertising) | 1 | Ranking Interpretation or whichever Article 1 angle the data supports | — |
| First publication for this industry | 1 | Industry Financial Health Snapshot | — |
| Major YoY movement in any industry | 1 | Year-over-Year Analysis | — |
| Exceptionally rich data (any industry) | 2 | Best Article 1 angle | Best Article 2 angle — pick for a different reader than Article 1 |

### Article checklist
- [ ] Select angle (1 or 2 based on industry)
- [ ] Draft article grounded in verified archive data — ECR numbers, named companies, driver data
- [ ] Publish on realrate.ai or news.realrate.ai
- [ ] Link to realrate.ai/rankings/[industry]/[year] within the article
- [ ] Share article on LinkedIn company page (separate post — content post, not a ranking data post)
