# Outreach SOP

---

## Purpose

To establish a consistent, repeatable process for all RealRate outreach activity — LinkedIn DMs, connection requests, and cold email campaigns. This SOP covers how to initiate contact, nurture leads, and move prospects toward a booked call, without pitching too early or damaging brand trust.

---

## 1. LinkedIn Outreach

### Objective
Convert LinkedIn content engagement and profile visits into qualified conversations with CFOs, risk officers, investors, and procurement leaders — without pitching in the first message.

### Best Practices
- Never pitch in the first message — build rapport first
- Personalise every message — reference something specific (their post, company, industry)
- Lead with insight or value, not a product pitch
- Keep messages short — 3–5 lines max
- Always follow up — most replies come from the 2nd or 3rd message
- Use Holger's personal page for high-value targets — his credibility opens doors faster

### Conversion Path
Content or post → prospect comments or reacts → Amneh or Holger DMs them → no pitch in first message → warm conversation → soft introduction to RealRate → booked call

### Connection Request
- Always include a personalised note (never blank)
- Reference something specific: their role, industry, or a post they made
- Keep it to 1–2 lines
- No pitch — just a reason to connect

**Template framework:**
> *"[Name], came across your [post/profile/comment] on [topic] — [one specific observation]. Would love to connect with others thinking about [relevant theme]."*

### First Message (Post-Connection)
- Reference why you connected
- Lead with a relevant insight or data point from RealRate
- Ask a genuine question — make it about them, not RealRate
- No links, no pitch, no CTA to book a call

**Template framework:**
> *"Thanks for connecting, [Name]. [Personalised observation about their work or industry]. We've been seeing [relevant RealRate finding] across [their industry] — curious if that aligns with what you're seeing on your end?"*

### Follow-Up Sequence

| Touch | Timing | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Message 1 | Day 1 post-connection | Insight + genuine question |
| Message 2 | Day 4–5 (if no reply) | Different angle — new data point or industry observation |
| Message 3 | Day 10–12 (if no reply) | Soft close — offer something of value (free report, archive link) |
| Message 4 | Day 20+ | Final touch — keep door open, no pressure |

### Qualifying a Lead
Before pitching or suggesting a call, confirm:
- They're in the right role (CFO, risk officer, investor, procurement)
- They've shown genuine interest (replied, asked a question, engaged with content)
- There's a clear product fit (which RealRate product solves their pain point)

### Moving to a Call
Only suggest a call after at least one genuine exchange. Keep it low-pressure:
> *"Happy to share the full [industry] data if useful — we could do a quick 20-minute walk-through if that's easier than back-and-forth here."*

## 2. Email Outreach (Instantly)

### Objective
Run targeted cold email campaigns to RealRate's ICPs — reaching CFOs, investors, supply chain leaders, and risk officers at scale while maintaining quality and brand integrity.

### Current Challenges
- Reply rates are low and replies are largely uninterested
- Sequences need a full strategic rework — ICP targeting, messaging, and tone

### Best Practices
- Tone should feel like talking to a peer, not a sales pitch
- Personalise at the ICP level minimum — ideally at the company level
- Include pain point, value proposition, social proof, and a clear CTA in every sequence
- Test with 100 leads before scaling — never launch at full volume untested
- Always A/B test subject lines and email body
- Monitor bounce rate closely — keep below 10%

### Campaign Setup Process
1. Define ICP for the campaign (which of the 6 ICPs, which industry)
2. Build or source lead list — verified emails only
3. Write sequence (see structure below)
4. Set up A/B variants (at minimum 2 subject line variants)
5. Launch to 100 leads first
6. Monitor metrics for 5–7 days before scaling
7. Adjust based on results — do not scale underperforming campaigns

### Email Sequence Structure (Per Campaign)

| Email | Timing | Goal | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Day 1 | Open + first impression | Peer-to-peer, data-led hook |
| Email 2 | Day 3–4 | Build interest | Different angle — insight or pain point |
| Email 3 | Day 7–8 | Social proof | Bayerische testimonial or industry finding |
| Email 4 | Day 12–14 | Soft CTA | Low-pressure call offer |
| Email 5 | Day 20–22 | Final touch | Keep door open, no pressure |

### Email Structure Per Message
- **Subject line:** Short, specific, curiosity-driven — no clickbait
- **Opening:** Personalised observation — their industry, role, or a relevant data point
- **Body:** Pain point → RealRate value proposition → one specific, verifiable claim
- **CTA:** One clear action — never more than one ask per email
- **Signature:** Professional, minimal

### ICP-Specific Messaging Anchors

| ICP | Lead With | Value Proposition |
|---|---|---|
| Top-Rated Companies | Financial transparency as a trust signal | Seal + free evaluation + industry benchmarking |
| Non Top-Rated | Gap between perceived and actual financial health | Consulting + clear path to improvement |
| SME / Private Equity | Lack of credible third-party validation | Seal + report + workshop |
| Investors | Unreliable or conflicted rating sources | Independent database + AI-generated reports |
| Supply Chain | Hidden financial risk in supplier base | Early warning system — flag risk 12 months out |

### Handling Replies

| Reply Type | Response Approach |
|---|---|
| Interested | Respond within 24 hours — personalise, move toward a call |
| Unsubscribe / stop | Remove immediately, no follow-up |
| Not the right person | Ask for a referral to the right contact |
| Negative / annoyed | Acknowledge professionally, remove from sequence |
| No reply after all 5 | Move to a cold nurture list — re-engage in 60–90 days with new angle |

### Fixing Low Reply Rates — Immediate Actions
1. Review subject lines — are they specific and curiosity-driven?
2. Review opening lines — are they personalised or generic?
3. Review ICP list quality — are contacts in the right role and industry?
4. Shorten emails — if over 150 words, cut
5. Test a more direct, data-led hook in Email 1
6. Review sending domain health — check deliverability scores

## 3. Analytics & Reporting (Outreach)

### Objective
Track outreach performance to identify what's working, fix what isn't, and continuously improve reply rates, interested rates, and booked calls.

### Tools
| Tool | What to Track |
|---|---|
| Instantly | Bounce rate, open rate, reply rate, interested rate, booked call rate |
| LinkedIn | DM reply rate, connection acceptance rate, profile views from outreach |

### Target Metrics (Email)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Bounce Rate | ~5%, max 10% |
| Open Rate | 40–60% |
| Reply Rate | ~6% (including negative) |
| Interested Rate | ~1% |
| Call Booked Rate | 0.66% |

### Target Metrics (LinkedIn)
*[To be defined — currently establishing baseline]*

### Reporting Cadence
- **Weekly:** Check bounce rate, open rate, reply rate per active campaign
- **Monthly:** Full review — interested rate, booked calls, sequence performance per ICP
- **Per campaign:** Post-100-lead review before scaling

### How to Act on Findings

| Signal | Action |
|---|---|
| Bounce rate > 10% | Pause campaign — check list quality and domain health |
| Open rate < 30% | Test new subject lines — current ones not working |
| Reply rate < 3% | Rewrite Email 1 — hook or personalisation failing |
| Interested rate = 0% | Review ICP fit and value proposition messaging |
| High open, low reply | Body copy issue — message not resonating after the subject line |
| Good reply, no calls booked | CTA issue — ask is too big or too vague |
