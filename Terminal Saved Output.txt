  ### Post 2 — Thu Mar 12 · P3 · Let Me Explain · AIDA                                                                         
  ```                                                                                                                          
  A company can be liquid and insolvent at the same time.                                                                      
  Most people in finance treat these as the same risk. They are not.                                                           
                                                                                                                               
  Liquidity answers one question: can you pay your bills today?                                                                
  Solvency answers a different one: is the value of what you own greater than what you owe?                                    
                                                                                                                               
  You can have cash in the bank and still be structurally underwater.                                                          
  You can be illiquid and still have a fortress balance sheet.                                                                 
                                                                                                                               
  The ECR measures solvency — specifically, the ratio of company valuation to total assets.                                    
  In every sector we have ranked, the companies that survive distress are almost always the ones with strong solvency ratios,  
  regardless of what their liquidity looked like in the months before.                                                         
                                                                                                                               
  The distinction rarely comes up until a company is already in trouble.                                                       
  By then, it is usually too late to act on it.
  ```                                                                                                                          

  ### Post 6 — Thu Mar 26 · P3 · Let Me Explain · What/So What/Now What                                                        
  ```                                                                                                                          
  Causal AI and predictive AI are not the same thing.                                                                          
  In financial health assessment, the difference is the whole point.                                                           
                                                                                                                               
  Predictive AI finds patterns in historical data and extrapolates forward.                                                    
  It tells you what usually happens next based on what happened before.                                                        
                                                                                                                               
  Causal AI asks a different question — not what comes next, but what actually caused this outcome.                            
  Which specific variables drove it. In what proportion. And why.                                                              
                                                                                                                               
  In practice: a predictive model might flag that companies with declining revenue tend to become distressed.                  
  A causal model identifies which balance sheet drivers — equity ratio, liability structure, cash-flow volatility — are        
  actually responsible, and by how much.                                                                                       
                                                                                                                               
  RealRate uses causal AI because the goal is not to predict distress.                                                         
  It is to explain the drivers of stability so that decision-makers can act on them.                                           
  Explainability is what makes it useful, not just interesting.                                                                
                                                                                                                               
  Do you think explainability is a genuine priority in how your organisation uses AI —                                         
  or is it mostly a compliance requirement?                                                                                    
  ```
